By\u00a0Kyla Guilfoil\u00a0and\u00a0Caroline Kenny<\/p>\n

President Joe Biden told reporters Wednesday there is \u201cno question\u201d former President Donald Trump supported an insurrection, a day after the Colorado Supreme Court ruled that Trump was disqualified from the state\u2019s 2024 primary ballot because of his role in the Jan. 6, 2021, attack on the Capitol.<\/p>\n

\u201cWell I think certainly it\u2019s self-evident,” Biden said. “You saw it all. Now whether the 14th Amendment applies, I\u2019ll let the court make that decision. But he certainly supported an insurrection.”<\/p>\n

\u201cNo question about it \u2014 none, zero. And he seems to be doubling down on everything,\u201d the president added.<\/p>\n

In its ruling Tuesday, the Colorado Supreme Court\u00a0declared\u00a0that the former president \u201cis disqualified from holding the office of President under\u00a0Section Three of the Fourteenth Amendment\u00a0of the United States Constitution,\u201d and thus should not be on the state\u2019s primary ballot next year.<\/p>\n

The bombshell ruling is the first of its kind, finding its basis in a lawsuit that focused on a little-known\u00a0provision in the 14th Amendment.\u00a0None of numerous similar challenges in other states have been successful so far.<\/p>\n

The Colorado Supreme Court explicitly included in its opinion that that state’s secretary of state, Jena Griswold, \u201cmay not list President Trump\u2019s name on the 2024 presidential primary ballot, nor may she count any write-in votes cast for him.\u201d<\/p>\n
