Democrats say the emails were routine and that Hunter Biden\u2019s business associate worked as Joe Biden\u2019s financial adviser and helped the then-vice president file his tax returns.<\/p>\n

By\u00a0Tom Winter<\/p>\n

Joe Biden exchanged emails with his son Hunter\u2019s business associate\u00a054 times while he was serving as vice president, and some of the messages were sent around the time\u00a0the elder Biden was traveling to Ukraine and his son was working for a Ukrainian gas company, according to records released by House Republicans.<\/p>\n

A White House spokesperson declined to comment but pointed to\u00a0statements by House Democrats that Hunter Biden\u2019s business associate, Eric Schwerin, worked as Joe Biden\u2019s financial adviser from 2009 to 2017 and helped him file his tax returns. Schwerin did not respond to requests for comment.<\/p>\n

The records released describe the dates of the emails and who exchanged them, but do not include the messages themselves. NBC News has not reviewed the contents of the emails.<\/p>\n

All told, then-Vice President Biden emailed Schwerin 35 times before and after his 2014 trips to Ukraine. Five emails were exchanged just prior to a trip by Vice President Biden to Ukraine on June 7, 2014, and 27 emails were sent from the end of June to a second trip to Ukraine by Biden on Nov. 21, 2014.<\/p>\n

The bulk of the communication occurred in the lead-up to and following an agreement for Hunter Biden to serve on the board of the Ukrainian natural gas company Burisma.<\/p>\n

Earlier this year, Rep. Jamie Raskin, D-Md., said in a news release that Schwerin told House Ways and Means Committee staff in a March interview that the elder Biden was not involved in the business of his son or any of his relatives.<\/p>\n

Raskin said Schwerin stated that \u201che was not aware of any involvement by President Biden in the financial conduct of the President\u2019s relatives\u2019 businesses, much less any transactions into or out of the then-vice president\u2019s bank account related to business conducted by any Biden family member.\u201d<\/p>\n

The email disclosures were part of law enforcement records released by House Republicans following the testimony of IRS whistleblower Joseph Ziegler. This month, Ziegler provided the documents to the House Ways and Means Committee\u00a0in conjunction with a sworn affidavit. The committee, which also heard the testimony of a second IRS whistleblower, Gary Shapley, is led by Rep. Jason Smith, R-Mo.<\/p>\n

Ziegler told Congress that \u201cthere were multiple emails found with Hunter Biden and his business associates\u201d but the investigative team was unaware at the time that then-Vice President Biden used an alias in his private emails.<\/p>\n
