A former Trump White House aide has asked a federal judge to dismiss a lawsuit that alleged he violated state and federal laws in connection with the online publication of\u00a0laptop content\u00a0attributed to Hunter Biden.<\/p>\n

The president\u2019s son\u00a0sued Garrett Ziegler and his company, Marco Polo, in September, claiming they broke state and federal laws in an effort to create an online searchable database with 128,000 emails attributed to Biden.<\/p>\n

Attorneys for Ziegler, who worked on trade policy in the White House under Trump adviser\u00a0Peter Navarro, argued in a 30-page filing that Biden\u2019s legal team had not established a causal connection between Ziegler\u2019s conduct and Biden\u2019s claims of injury related to the data and that they failed to state “a viable claim.”<\/p>\n

\u201cPlaintiff alleges no facts which demonstrate Defendants ever accessed any computer, storage, or service which Plaintiff either owns or has exclusive control over,\u201d Ziegler’s attorneys wrote in a filing with a federal court in the Central District of California.<\/p>\n

In their lawsuit, Biden’s attorneys had acknowledged that “the precise manner by which Defendant Ziegler obtained Plaintiff\u2019s data remains unclear” but said it was indisputable that Ziegler and his company had “to at least some extent, accessed, tampered with, manipulated, altered, copied and damaged” data attributed to Biden in a manner that was illegal and without his consent.<\/p>\n
