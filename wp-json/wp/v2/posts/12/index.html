(The Center Square) \u2013 It wasn\u2019t Santa Claus delivering Christmas presents, but on Thursday afternoon, voter advocacy group Let\u2019s Go Washington turned in 419,699 signatures for Initiative 2109, which would repeal the capital gains tax, and 437,960 signatures for Initiative 2111, which would prohibit income taxes at the city and county level.<\/p>\n

\u201cIt\u2019s a lot,\u201d deadpanned Glen Morgan, founder of the \u201cWe The Governed\u201d website, when asked to comment on the turn-in of signatures at the Secretary of State\u2019s office in Tumwater.<\/p>\n

Let\u2019s Go Washington founder Brian Heywood of Redmond spoke about his motivation for the two initiatives.<\/p>\n

\n
\n

\u201cWhy am I doing this?\u201d he asked. \u201cBecause I grew up poor. I grew up poor in Arizona, and I believe\u2026that the \u2018American Dream\u2019 is real and it\u2019s the coolest freakin\u2019 thing on Earth. It doesn\u2019t matter where you\u2019re born or how poor you are or what station in life or what your status is, if you want to, you can find opportunities in this fantastic country to do things that no one else can do and you can change your station.\u201d<\/p>\n

Washington voters have directly voted down an income tax 10 times at the ballot box over the past 100 years, in 1934, 1936, 1938, 1942, 1944, 1970, 1973, 1975, 1982 and 2010.<\/p>\n

\u201cHow many times do voters need to tell lawmakers to stop with income taxes once and for all?\u201d Heywood said. \u201cOnce politicians\u2019 budgets take a hit from people like Bezos taking their money to Florida, who do you think will be the target of the next big tax? Small business owners, homeowners, young entrepreneurs, hard-working restaurant and family farm employers and regular, hardworking taxpayers.\u201d<\/p>\n

Heywood was making reference to billionaire Amazon founder Jeff Bezos announcing in November that he plans to\u00a0move<\/strong>\u00a0from Washington to Florida to be closer to his parents.<\/p>\n

In January 2022, Washington implemented a 7% tax on the sale of financial assets\u00a0despite a legal challenge. Earlier this year, the state Supreme Court upheld the constitutionality of the capital gains tax.<\/p>\n

\u201cWell, I mean this is a Christmas present for all taxpayers of Washington state,\u201d initiatives sponsor Rep. Jim Walsh, R-Aberdeen, and chair of the Washington State Republican Party, told The Center Square.<\/p>\n

He said ordinary people aren\u2019t buying the narrative of the capital gains tax proponents that the rich aren\u2019t paying their fair share of taxes.<\/p>\n

\u201cThey know the capital gains tax is an income tax, and they know there\u2019s a slippery slope,\u201d Walsh continued. \u201cThey\u2019re going to keep a high threshold initially.\u201d<\/p>\n

That\u2019s a reference to at least one Democrat \u2013 Sen. Jamie Pedersen, D-Seattle \u2013 saying in emails and at at least one public meeting that he would like to\u00a0leverage<\/strong>\u00a0a capital gains tax into a general income tax in Washington.<\/p>\n

Invest in Washington Now, which describes itself as a progressive revenue solutions organization, decried the signature-gathering efforts.<\/p>\n

\u201cI-2109 would repeal the capital gains tax on the super-rich, taking more than\u00a0$900 million\u00a0from education and child care funding to line the pockets of WA\u2019s wealthiest,\u201d Invest in Washington Now Executive Director Treasure Mackley said in an email blast.<\/p>\n

She went on to claim, \u201cI-2111 would slash benefits that Washingtonians are counting on, like paid family leave and long-term care benefits.\u201d<\/p>\n

Initiatives are required to receive nearly 325,000 verified signatures to qualify for the ballot.<\/p>\n

Assuming the two initiatives qualify with enough valid signatures, lawmakers in the Democratic-controlled Legislature have several options: pass them during the upcoming legislative session; take no action and let them go to the ballot; or pass alternative proposals, which would see both initiatives and the alternative proposals placed on the ballot at the next state general election in 2024.<\/p>\n

Let\u2019s Go Washington has previously turned in signatures for initiatives to repeal Washington\u2019s cap-and-trade program; provide parents and guardians with a right to review educational materials, receive certain notifications, and opt out of certain sexual health education programs; and remove certain restrictions on police officers\u2019 ability to engage in vehicular pursuits.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
