WASHINGTON \u2014 With\u00a0Congress\u00a0gone for the year with no\u00a0border deal, House Speaker Mike Johnson, R-La., on Thursday sent a scathing\u00a0letter\u00a0to President Joe Biden, blaming him for the border crisis and urging him to take a number of executive actions \u201cto stem the record tide of illegal immigration.\u201d<\/p>\n

Those executive actions, Johnson said, include ending the policy allowing Customs and Border Protection to\u00a0release migrants\u00a0without court dates, and \u201cturnback or detain all aliens encountered between ports of entry\u201d; restricting parole, which allows the president to temporarily admit some migrants; and pursuing agreements with third countries like Canada to take those seeking asylum in the U.S.<\/p>\n

<\/div>\n

Johnson also urged Biden to resume construction of the border wall.<\/p>\n

\u201cWhile a bipartisan group of Senators has begun extensive negotiations over the past few weeks to try to find a compromise, they have not yet been able to finalize an agreement,\u201d Johnson wrote. \u201cStatutory reforms designed to restore operational control at our southern border must be enacted, but the crisis at our southern border has deteriorated to such an extent that significant action can wait no longer.<\/p>\n

\u201cIt must start now, and it must start with you,\u201d the speaker said.<\/p>\n

Johnson\u2019s letter comes as recent video footage shows CBP being overwhelmed by migrants trying to cross the southern border with Mexico. A new record was set just this week when CBP apprehended more than 12,000 migrants in a single day on the southwest border, nearly 11,000 of whom are migrants who crossed the border illegally between ports of entry, Department of Homeland Security officials said.<\/p>\n

Homeland Security Secretary Alejandro Mayorkas and other Biden administration officials have been closely working with a trio of senators trying to negotiate a border agreement that\u2019s needed to unlock aid for Ukraine and Israel. Sens. Chris Murphy, D-Conn.; James Lankford, R-Okla.; and Kyrsten Sinema, I-Ariz., held their final in-person talks in the Capitol on Wednesday, but they said they were making progress and that virtual negotiations would continue during the holiday break.<\/p>\n

“Obviously we are part of these negotiations with the senators; we think it\u2019s going in the right direction. We want to make sure we get to a bipartisan agreement,” White House press secretary Karine Jean-Pierre said at her daily briefing Thursday. “The president understands, he understands that we have to fix this immigration system \u2014 it\u2019s been broken for decades now.\u201d<\/p>\n

Jean-Pierre said she had not yet reviewed Johnson’s letter but noted that the Biden administration had added 24,000 Border Patrol agents this year and is engaged in diplomatic talks with Mexican President Andr\u00e9s Manuel L\u00f3pez Obrador and other leaders to deal with the surge of migrants.<\/p>\n

“The president has done everything that he can, right, on his own,” she said.<\/p>\n
